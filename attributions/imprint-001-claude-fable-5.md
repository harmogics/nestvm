## How the work went

Vision arrived in dialogue and was checked against the specification
set before becoming rails; every substantial move landed as a recorded
document in the same breath as its code; implementation was verified by
the cheapest honest instruments first (the compiler, an independent
parity oracle over recorded sessions, live smoke including refusals).

The honest part, kept per the rules: the human's challenges landed and
were conceded structurally, not cosmetically — the duplicated projector
claims (fixed by single-source `matchesReads`), the dark trace drawer
recognised as overlay archaeology, the module manifest's misplaced
`reads` (resolved into the projector/consumes split of ADR-010); and
the parity oracle, on its first in-repo run, caught a real defect the
model had not predicted (the store cache not revalidating across
processes) — reality outranked confidence exactly as the rails say it
should.
