# Ecosystem guilde manifests

**Status:** working orientation · non-normative.

This directory holds high-level manifests for classes of ecosystem projects
that place Nest VM at their executable centre and grow a human, institutional,
and contextual environment around it. A manifest describes a way of composing
such an environment: its purpose, inhabitants, movements, responsibility
thresholds, possible publications, and open growth vectors.

The spelling **guilde** names this ecosystem-level family and distinguishes it
from a capability **guild** in [`capabilities/`](../capabilities/README.md). A
guilde manifest is not a capability seed, package manifest, HUID module
manifest, assembly configuration, or Nest VM extension specification. It may
orient many of those artefacts in an independently governed project, but it
installs nothing and grants no authority.

## Register

| Manifest | Scope | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [`vision_guilde.md`](./vision_guilde.md) | The common vision of an ecosystem growing with people around a Nest VM centre | VISION |

Possible later manifests include `marketplace.md`, `analytics.md`,
`finops.md`, and other application fields. Each may disclose a different
environmental figure without turning its vocabulary into machine primitives.

## Common reading discipline

A guilde manifest should make visible:

1. the field of purpose around the machine;
2. the people and other participants who inhabit it;
3. the material gathered and the movements made over it;
4. the publications that may return from Nest VM into the environment;
5. the human thresholds for revision, adoption, refusal, and attestation;
6. the ways the environment may grow, branch, or remain deliberately thin;
7. the boundary between local experience and anything offered to a commons.

These are questions of orientation, not a closed schema. The Nest Runtime
Specification Set remains the authority for what the machine is; an external
project remains the authority for its own purposes, policy, and lifecycle. The
semantic relationship between independently governed projects belongs at the
[bridge](../BRIDGE.md), not inside either project's core.
