# The vision guilde — growing an environment around a Nest VM centre

**Status:** VISION · working orientation · non-normative · 2026-07-22.

This manifest describes the common vision by which an ecosystem project may
place Nest VM at its executable centre and grow around it an environment that
has a centre of its own: human purpose, situated material, relationships,
responsibility, and a history of adopted forms. It is the general member of a
family that may later include marketplace, analytics, FinOps, and other guilde
manifests.

It does not define a product, prescribe an assembly, or widen the Nest VM
contract. It describes a recurring movement and the forks through which
different projects may disclose their own form.

## 1. A centre within a living centre

Nest VM is not the whole environment. It is the place where declared figures
can move, accumulate, gather, publish, and leave an inspectable history. Around
that machine centre, a project forms another centre from what the machine must
not universalise: why the work matters, who participates, what may be brought
near, which consequences are acceptable, and what a community is prepared to
adopt.

```text
the project's field
  people · purpose · material · relations · responsibility
                  ↓ frames and supplies
        declared movements through Nest VM
                  ↓ publishes and returns
  readings · candidates · refusals · open questions
                  ↓ human judgement
       the environment revises and grows
```

The environment is therefore not a decorative shell around a sovereign
machine. Nor is Nest VM a hidden implementation detail owned by the
environment. Each retains its centre. Their meeting is a repeated, attributable
exchange in which the human is constitutive rather than peripheral.

## 2. The recurring movement

The general algorithm is a cycle of formation, not a fixed sequence of product
steps:

1. **A field gathers.** A question, practice, obligation, opportunity, or body
   of material becomes consequential to particular people.
2. **A purpose is framed.** The environment names why movement is wanted, what
   belongs within the present scope, and which responsibility cannot be
   delegated.
3. **A figure is admitted.** A declared way of collecting, accumulating, and
   publishing is selected, adapted, or proposed without silently changing the
   machine that will carry it.
4. **The figure moves.** Nest VM records what entered, how readiness formed,
   what gathered, what crossed the membrane, and where the movement settled.
5. **A publication returns.** The environment receives a candidate, a refusal,
   an explicit unknown, an unfinished state, or another declared result — never
   a private answer presented as accomplished fact.
6. **A person takes responsibility.** The publication is read, revised,
   adopted, disputed, or left open through an attributable human act.
7. **The environment grows.** Adopted results may change local understanding,
   invite a new figure, connect people and material differently, or become a
   reviewed form available for later work.
8. **Experience may cross a threshold.** A project may keep the result private,
   promote it within its own scope, exchange it with another governed
   environment, or offer a reviewed derivative to a commons.

The cycle may stop honestly at any movement. Growth is not compulsory, and
recurrence does not imply automatic promotion.

## 3. The human place

The human does not merely provide input to a machine-built environment. People
bring purpose, consequential selection, lived contradiction, revision,
acceptance, and responsibility for what changes scope. Nest VM can make these
acts durable and addressable; it cannot replace their authority.

The environment also grows with the human in a second sense: its figures,
readings, roles, and boundaries may become more articulate through use. That
growth remains a fresh act. A successful history may propose a reusable form,
but cannot install itself as method or norm.

## 4. Forks through which a project discloses itself

A guilde manifest keeps several choices visible rather than fixing one
universal environment:

- **Point of entry:** a person may begin from a question, a corpus, a recurring
  practice, an event stream, a responsibility, or a desired change.
- **Radius:** the environment may belong to one practitioner, a team, an
  organisation, a market, a public field, or a relation among several of them.
- **Context formation:** material may be selected by a person, proposed by an
  algorithm, inherited from policy, or assembled through a reviewable mixture.
- **Cadence:** movement may be a bounded session, a recurring review, a
  continuing operational rhythm, or a trial separated from ordinary work.
- **Kind of return:** a publication may be an articulation, comparison,
  decision candidate, warning, plan, executable figure, or explicit account of
  why no result should yet be adopted.
- **Institutional weight:** a result may remain personal, inform a practice,
  enter a process, become a local standard, or fail the threshold between any
  two of these states.
- **Relation to the commons:** experience may remain private, travel between
  consenting environments, or be abstracted and published with provenance and
  rights intact.

These forks are analytical lenses. A particular guilde may privilege some of
them, introduce others, or state that a branch is outside its purpose.

## 5. A family of high-level manifests

The vision guilde supplies the common relation; later manifests may articulate
different fields around the same independent machine centre.

- A **marketplace guilde** might study how offers, needs, trust, admission, and
  exchange become inspectable without reducing a market to transactions alone.
- An **analytics guilde** might distinguish observation, interpretation,
  comparison, and consequential judgement while preserving their source seams.
- A **FinOps guilde** might relate cost, capability, value, uncertainty, and
  accountable choice without allowing optimisation to become unexamined
  authority.

These examples are directions, not definitions. Each environment supplies its
own language and governance. Nest VM supplies the common ability to carry
declared movement and return an inspectable publication.

## 6. Suggested anatomy of a guilde manifest

A future guilde manifest may describe, in its own order and vocabulary:

- the field and its purpose;
- its inhabitants and their irreducible responsibilities;
- the material and relationships from which context can be formed;
- the characteristic movements worth declaring;
- the publications the environment can recognise;
- the thresholds at which consequence or authority changes;
- the possible routes of growth and honest forms of non-growth;
- the boundary between private history, local adoption, exchange, and commons;
- the questions that must remain open until lived use supplies evidence.

This anatomy is deliberately not a template that every project must fill. It is
a set of angles from which an environment can discover whether it has made its
relationship with the machine, its people, and its consequences sufficiently
visible.

## 7. Guards

1. **A guilde is not a Nest VM ontology.** Its concepts remain the language of
   an environment unless a separate, evidence-backed widening establishes a
   methodology-independent machine obligation.
2. **A guilde manifest installs nothing.** It is an orientation for forming a
   project, not executable topology, capability, configuration, or permission.
3. **The environment does not own the machine's truth.** It may interpret and
   adopt publications, but it does not rewrite committed history or redefine
   Nest VM conformance.
4. **The machine does not own the environment's purpose.** It carries declared
   figures; it does not decide what a community should value or institutionalise.
5. **Growth remains attributable.** New figures, roles, policies, and public
   derivatives enter through explicit human and organisational thresholds.
6. **A bridge is semantic, not capture.** Independently governed projects may
   inform one another while retaining separate code, authority, lifecycle, and
   the right to refuse a proposed change.

The intended result is neither one universal ecosystem nor a collection of
unrelated shells. It is a field of distinct environments able to grow around a
shared kind of executable centre, meet through explicit boundaries, and learn
from one another without collapsing their responsibilities.
