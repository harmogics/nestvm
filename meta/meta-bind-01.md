## 1. The principle

The system assembles as a set of parts, and every load-bearing part is
delineated by the same three phases:

- **collection** — what may enter, declared in advance (rules, claims,
  manifests, shelves: the entry is never ambient accident);
- **accumulation** — the transformation that forms (winding, folding,
  judging, integrating: where understanding grows);
- **publication** — the formed result released under a declared form
  (a committed tuple, a snapshot, a rendering, an attested document:
  never a private return).

Geometries differ — per unit, per layer, per radius — but the figure
does not. The system as a whole instantiates it, and so do its parts,
self-similarly, down to the vocabulary of its packages.

## 3. The degenerate rule

The machine already licenses thin instances: a knot's minimal form "is a
degenerate configuration of the same accumulator, never a different kind
of unit" (Vol. 05). So a module's `select` is an accumulation of zero
depth, and a materialised reading (an archive download) is a publication
whose accumulation is identity. Degeneracy is named, never hidden: a part
missing a phase is either *degenerate* (and says so) or *off the rails*
(and must justify itself).

## 4. What the principle powers

1. **The fractal test** (Art. 12; FORMAT §2.3): for any addition, name its
   three parts; a piece whose parts cannot be named is off the rails —
   the cheapest drift detector the system has.
2. **Placement**: the three parts locate a fragment in the tree —
   collection declarations sit with the wire and the manifests;
   accumulation sits server-side (machine, plane); publication is a
   declared form on the wire or a rendering (ADR-007 routing).
3. **Review**: the collection face is always an inspectable declaration
   (rules, claims, manifests) — "declared form, supplied content" is this
   figure's first phase stated as law (Vol. 01 §2.5).

## 5. Guards — what this principle is not

1. **Not a universal ontology.** PHILOSOPHY's non-goals bind here: this
   is the construction rule of *this* system — chosen, checkable, and
   binding as a review question, never a claim about knowledge at large.
2. **Not an authority.** A meta-bind is a derived reading; the layers'
   normative texts govern, and this document is corrected where they
   disagree (README, authority rule).
3. **Not symmetry-forcing.** A genuinely two- or four-phase mechanism is
   not to be contorted into three. The honest outcomes are exactly:
   name it degenerate (§3), revise this principle by a dated note, or
   place the part outside the figure deliberately, with its reason
   recorded.
