## 1. The principle

Between altitudes, movement is two one-way flows:

- **upward — experience.** The particular ripens into the general only
  by abstraction: a principle proven in one case is *restated without
  the case* and lands in the governing layer (a volume section, an
  article, a format rule). The case itself retires, pinned to its
  record.
- **downward — rails.** The general reaches the particular only as
  constraints — guidelines, formats, registration sites — never as an
  edit made for one case's sake.

A general document therefore never depends on a particular artefact to
be understood or to govern; a particular artefact always names the
rails it ran on.

## 3. Guards

1. **Provenance is not dependence.** A general text may *credit* the
   particular that taught it — a dated amendment note, a ledger line, an
   imprint — but must never *need* it. Crediting names history;
   depending imports it.
2. **Abstraction is restatement, not reference.** The general lands
   stated cleanly; a general document that cites a case to complete its
   own meaning has not finished abstracting.
3. **Not symmetry-forcing.** Some experience never generalises — it
   stays an imprint; some rails have no case yet — they stand as
   declared. Both are honest states, not failures of the figure.
