# Nest VM — Vectors for a Methodology-Hosting Architecture

**Status:** Working orientation. This text identifies architectural vectors that require continued study if Nest VM is to host CoAgnes, The Algorithmic Company, and other methodology-bearing systems without encoding any one of them as the machine’s hidden ontology.

It does not prescribe a final architecture. Each vector names a field of tension, possible branches, and the distinctions that should remain visible while experience determines which forms deserve promotion.

## 1. The central proposition

Nest VM should not become a methodology expressed in software. It should become a machine in which methodologies can be declared, executed, examined, revised, compared, and transferred without silently acquiring machine-level authority.

Its stable centre is therefore deliberately narrower than CoAgnes or The Algorithmic Company:

- an append-only wave log of attributable acts;
- declared figures composed from activation knots, bind descriptors, and output controllers;
- explicit readiness, rendezvous, publication, and settlement;
- a membrane separating internal formation from external capability;
- derived readings rather than surface-owned semantic state;
- human responsibility at consequential thresholds;
- honest incompleteness as an observable result.

Around this centre, several architectural vectors remain open. Their purpose is not to delay commitment indefinitely, but to let different implementations and product experiences disclose which distinctions are genuinely load-bearing.

## 2. Machine invariants versus methodological grammar

The first vector concerns what belongs to Nest VM itself and what should arrive through a methodology-bearing package.

One branch keeps the kernel extremely small: the log, propagation, knots, binds, controllers, settlement, and a minimal authoring grammar. CoAgnes then supplies its own fact families, figures, rubrics, roles, readings, and promotion protocols entirely as packages and extensions.

Another branch allows the machine to recognise a limited set of higher-order constructs—Charters, obligations, trials, assessments, or promotion states—where repeated experience shows that they are not merely CoAgnes concepts but recurring execution forms.

A third branch treats such constructs as named profiles: more stable than an ordinary package, but still outside the universal kernel.

The question is not whether a construct is useful. It is whether removing its domain vocabulary still leaves a machine-level obligation that multiple methodologies must satisfy.

Nest VM should widen only when that obligation can be stated independently of the methodology that first revealed it.

## 3. Executable figures versus living practice

A methodology may be represented as a pipeline, a graph, a recursive figure, a collection of gestures, or a family of situational patterns. No single representation should be assumed to exhaust the practice from which it arose.

One branch treats a figure as a relatively complete executable programme: declared topology, schemas, gates, services, and result contracts.

Another treats it as a partial scaffold whose missing content and local decisions are supplied during a run.

A further branch allows several figures to compete or cooperate around the same purpose, preserving alternative ways of reaching a result.

The architecture should therefore distinguish:

- the declared form of a method;
- the situated content supplied during one application;
- the trace of what actually happened;
- the interpretation formed from that trace;
- the reviewed derivative considered reusable afterwards.

Portability should preserve useful structure without claiming that lived practice has been completely captured.

## 4. Predeclared safety versus generative authoring

Nest VM requires declared form, yet CoAgnes depends on discovering structures that could not all have been known in advance.

Several authoring depths may coexist:

1. selection of a precompiled figure;
2. parameterisation of a declared template;
3. bounded `unfold`, where a semantic service supplies breadth and content inside a fixed form;
4. proposal of new topology as an inert package;
5. compilation and isolated trial;
6. reviewed admission into a local or shared library.

These are not merely permission levels. They are different kinds of act and should remain distinguishable in the log and the surface.

The open question is how far a running wave may participate in forming its successors. Nest VM may allow extensive self-description and self-proposal while retaining one hard boundary: a proposal cannot grant itself capability, install its own controller, or promote itself into organisational authority.

## 5. Historical truth versus epistemic qualification

The wave log can be authoritative about what was committed without being authoritative about the world described inside each tuple.

One branch keeps epistemic qualification entirely within domain schemas.

Another introduces shared envelopes for provenance, evidence references, confidence, dispute, supersession, and attestation.

A third uses orthogonal derived readings: the same committed history may be examined as observation, interpretation, assessment, or institutional adoption without multiplying causal authorities.

The architecture should leave room for different epistemic vocabularies while preserving a minimum distinction between:

- observed or externally attested events;
- human assertions;
- deterministic derivations;
- model proposals;
- accepted integrations;
- disputed, superseded, or explicitly unknown claims.

This distinction is critical for CoAgnes, where organisational adoption must never be inferred merely from the existence or fluency of a publication.

## 6. Context formation versus execution

The material presented to a figure is part of the computation. Yet context selection need not become part of the Nest VM execution kernel.

One branch lets a product or person assemble a source set directly.

Another uses Florispace-like graph neighbourhoods, semantic proximity, weighted relations, or policy constraints.

A further branch allows several context algorithms to produce competing manifests for comparison.

Whichever branch is used, execution should receive a sealed, attributable context manifest. Nest VM records what the run actually saw; it does not silently recompute the past when a graph, model, or retrieval policy changes.

This preserves a necessary distinction:

> context formation proposes the field of relevance; execution records the movement that occurred within the admitted field.

## 7. Accumulated semantic state versus replayable projection

An activation knot appears stateful because it accumulates understanding across time. Durable correctness, however, requires that this state remain reconstructable from committed acts.

One branch replays the complete relevant history whenever state is needed.

Another retains process-local executors as disposable caches.

A third introduces versioned, content-addressed wound snapshots carrying their source offsets, topology version, and rebuild rules.

These approaches may coexist at different scales. The invariant is not stateless implementation; it is the absence of unaccountable state.

A retained context window, model conversation, cached grade, or organisational assessment must never become a second truth whose origin cannot be reconstructed or challenged.

## 8. Readiness versus domain judgement

Nest VM requires readiness to move a wave, but no universal threshold can determine whether professional experience is sufficient, safe, or worthy of promotion.

One branch uses deterministic presence and structural conditions.

Another uses semantic evaluation against declared questions and thresholds.

Another uses domain-specific rubrics assessed by an independent evaluator.

A further branch requires several verdicts: structural readiness, evidential adequacy, human acceptance, and organisational promotion.

These verdicts should not collapse into one score. The machine may provide the rendezvous mechanics, while the methodology declares what is gathered, who may judge it, what evidence is required, and which consequences follow.

Readiness enables movement. It does not establish truth, usefulness, adoption, or completion.

## 9. Human participation versus automated movement

The machine should be capable of substantial autonomous propagation without obscuring the points at which responsibility changes hands.

Possible products may allocate work differently among people, models, deterministic services, and external agents. Some may require frequent human intervention; others may run long figures between review points.

The architecture should not enforce one interaction rhythm. It should enforce distinguishable authority.

Particular study is required around:

- purpose and Charter formation;
- consequential source selection;
- acceptance of generated topology;
- revision of published integrations;
- assessment independence;
- promotion into a guild, process, or organisational norm;
- attestation under a result contract.

The critical transfer remains `read → revise → append`: machine publication becomes human-adopted understanding only through a new attributable act. Different surfaces may realise this gesture differently, but none should replace it with silence or a hidden default.

## 10. Individual, process, and organisational carriers

The Algorithmic Company introduces Employee, Process, and Organization as three carriers of practice. They are powerful analytical lenses, but should not become fixed storage partitions or universal machine entities.

One branch represents carrier differences through actor roles and attribution lanes.

Another uses separate but connected figures at each scale.

Another treats transitions between carriers as explicit promotion or translation protocols.

A further branch permits other methodologies to define entirely different carrier structures.

Nest VM should support relations among scales without assuming that movement is linear. An individual articulation may influence a process without becoming a norm; a process may preserve obsolete understanding; an organisational rule may be challenged by situated expertise.

The machine should record these transfers and disagreements rather than forcing symmetry among the carriers.

## 11. Stable machine, plural surfaces

HUID should allow the same wave history to support a study workbench, an evidence ledger, a maturity cube, a professional guild space, a trial comparison, or a public figure explorer.

One branch favours generic schema-derived surfaces.

Another admits methodology-specific modules and visual grammars.

A third allows target organisations to compose their own devices from shared contracts and local modules.

The formation boundary remains critical: surfaces may select, focus, compare, and shape declared gestures, but they should not privately infer semantic results or hold durable meaning unavailable to replay.

The maturity cube is therefore one possible reading of CoAgnes history—not the geometry of the machine and not the ontology of the organisation.

## 12. Packages, seeds, and target-adaptive manifestation

Experience may travel as an executable pipeline package, a broader guild seed, a protocol profile, or a reviewed structural derivative.

One branch distributes only machine-portable figures.

Another allows packages to include schemas, rubrics, fixtures, projector declarations, and HUID module manifests.

Another keeps presentation local and transfers only semantic and execution forms.

The receiving system may reproduce the source experience closely or manifest it through its own geometry and policies. This makes admission more important than installation.

Every transferable artefact should state:

- provenance and licence;
- applicability and known failure conditions;
- required controllers and capabilities;
- fixtures and evaluation criteria;
- compatibility expectations;
- which parts may degrade generically;
- which missing parts must cause explicit refusal.

What travels is a proposition about useful structure, not authority over the receiving system.

## 13. Private experience versus the commons

CoAgnes requires precise internal attribution, while Harmogics requires a safe threshold through which selected structures may become public.

One branch publishes manually reviewed derivatives.

Another supports de-identification and rights review through declared figures.

Another allows organisations to exchange packages privately without entering a public commons.

A further branch may support federated comparison where structures are evaluated without centralising raw histories.

Nest VM should not decide which material is publishable. It should make the publication act explicit, attributable, reviewable, and technically separable from ordinary recording.

History is not publication. Structural similarity is not consent. Anonymisation is not automatically sufficient provenance.

## 14. Conformance versus product profiles

A Nest VM implementation may be deterministic, semantic, local, distributed, educational, organisational, or embedded inside another product.

The conformance surface should remain behavioural:

- compatible wave logs;
- compatible execution algorithms;
- compatible authoring formats;
- explicit extension profiles.

Products may then declare profiles such as Nest Education, CoAgnes organisational workbench, or Harmogics figure explorer without implying that their product vocabulary belongs to the universal machine.

This leaves open whether a future CoAgnes implementation is one application, a family of packages, an extension-conforming class, or an assembly profile. Experience should decide which boundary proves stable.

## 15. The governing test

For every proposed architectural commitment, the central question is:

> Does this commitment make a recurring obligation inspectable and portable, or does it freeze one currently preferred methodology into the machine?

A useful commitment should clarify at least one of:

- attribution;
- reachability;
- termination;
- provenance;
- authority;
- replayability;
- compatibility;
- honest failure.

If it merely makes one surface, maturity model, organisational vocabulary, or present implementation easier, it should probably remain a package, profile, or experiment.

Nest VM reaches the required flexibility not by becoming indefinitely abstract, but by keeping its invariant centre narrow and making every widening pass through evidence, trial, conformance, and human decision.

Its deepest promise is therefore not that any methodology can be represented without loss. It is that the forms, losses, translations, disagreements, and decisions involved in making a methodology executable can themselves remain visible.
