# Nest VM philosophy

**Status:** public foundation. This document states the commitments that make
the project coherent. It is not the normative machine contract; that contract
is the [Nest Runtime Specification Set](./specifications/00-introduction.md).

Nest VM is a specification-defined machine for forming, executing, and
examining accountable movements of thought. Its centre is not a canonical
codebase. Its centre is a shared contract: an append-only wave log, the
algorithms that move a wave through knots, binds, and the membrane, and the
portable authoring formats that declare figures of work.

## 1. The specification precedes every implementation

There is no single standard core from which all Nest VM systems must descend.
Implementations may differ in language, code structure, storage, deployment,
and the deterministic or semantic functions installed at assembly.

They meet as Nest VM implementations only where their observable behaviour
meets the specification. Compatibility has three surfaces:

1. the persisted log and protocol vocabulary;
2. the propagation, readiness, rendezvous, membrane, and settle algorithms;
3. the YAML core-structure formats and their compilation rules.

A reference implementation is evidence that the contract can be realised. It
is not the sovereign source of the contract, and replacing its code does not
replace the machine.

## 2. One history does not mean one unquestionable truth

The wave log is the authoritative history of what was committed: which act
occurred, under which key and correlation, and in which order. It does not make
every statement inside a tuple true about the world.

Observed events, human assertions, deterministic derivations, model proposals,
accepted integrations, disputes, and explicit unknowns remain distinguishable.
Correction never rewrites history; it appends a new, attributable act that
refers to what it corrects. Readings and indexes may be rebuilt. Committed
history is not silently repaired to match a later interpretation.

## 3. A gesture is more important than a screen event

A semantically meaningful human act becomes either a committed fact or an
authoring act that introduces declared topology. Pure navigation remains a
parameter of reading and does not pretend to be part of the machine's causal
history.

This boundary allows a surface to stay quiet while the underlying history stays
inspectable. The interface is a projection of the log and a factory for
declared gestures, never a second store of meaning.

## 4. Declared form, supplied content

Nest VM separates the form of an operation from the content supplied to it.
Knots declare what they may hear. Binds declare what they gather, judge, and
publish. Controllers declare which intentions they may discharge. A model,
agent, function, or person may supply content inside those forms without
silently changing the forms themselves.

This is the central discipline of semantic execution: generative capability is
useful precisely because its authority is bounded and inspectable.

## 5. The machine may participate in writing itself

A Nest system may read specifications and requirements, analyse observed work,
and propose new tuples, schemas, YAML packages, bind functions, controllers,
HUID modules, or external agents. The same workbench may therefore participate
in producing new levels of the system that operates it.

Proposal is not installation. Generated topology is inert until compiled and
admitted. Generated code is an artefact until reviewed, tested, and installed
through an authorised control-plane act. A running wave cannot grant itself a
new controller, credential, or capability merely by emitting a fact.

Self-writing is consequently a governed movement through specification,
evidence, trial, and human decision — not unrestricted self-modification.

## 6. Human responsibility does not disappear into automation

The machine may propose direction, unfold a figure, integrate material, expose
contradictions, or identify an honest stall. The human remains responsible for
purpose, consequential source selection, judgement, revision, acceptance, and
attestation.

The decisive transfer is **read → revise → append**: a person encounters a
published integration, makes their changes and reasons visible, and commits a
new attributable result. Silence is not consent. A bind completing is not the
same event as a human adopting what it produced.

## 7. Honest incompleteness is a valid result

Quiescence is not completion, fluency is not understanding, and a high grade is
not truth. A figure may settle while remaining unfinished. A knot may be marked
unknown. A controller may fail. A budget may end before a useful integration
forms.

These are observable states of the work, not defects to conceal behind a calm
interface. Completion exists only where a declared result contract and the
required human judgement say that it exists.

## 8. Figures are portable, contextual, and revisable

YAML packages carry reusable figures of movement between implementations and
products. They can preserve a useful way of asking, gathering, checking,
integrating, and publishing without freezing one implementation or one model
inside the method.

A figure is never universal merely because it worked once. It carries version,
provenance, applicability, fixtures, and known failure conditions. Reuse enters
fresh context and fresh admission; precedent informs a new act but does not
silently become law.

An ecosystem project may place Nest VM at its executable centre and grow around
it a centre of its own: people, purpose, context, policy, and responsibility.
High-level [guilde manifests](./manifests/README.md) may orient that growth
without making the environment's vocabulary part of the machine contract. The
machine carries declared movement; the surrounding project and its people
remain responsible for why that movement matters and what it may change.

## 9. Experience reaches the commons through a threshold

A raw log belongs to the context in which people and systems produced it. It
may contain personal attribution, confidential material, provider output, and
evidence whose rights do not travel with the log.

What may enter a public commons is a reviewed derivative: a de-identified
structure, a consented example, or a versioned figure with explicit provenance
and licence. Internal attribution may remain precise while a public pattern is
generalised. Publication is an additional human and organisational act, never
a side effect of recording.

## 10. Growth is deliberate widening

New capability grows on declared rails. If an observed need does not fit the
current contracts, the honest result is a proposed widening: its motivation,
semantics, compatibility effect, enforcement path, and verification arrive
together.

Nest VM is therefore neither a fixed product nor an indefinitely permissive
framework. It is a specification plus a workbench for making change visible,
testable, portable, and attributable.

## Non-goals

Nest VM is not:

- a claim to a universal ontology of knowledge;
- a canonical implementation that all systems must fork;
- a reason to collect every movement of human attention;
- an automatic route from one person's trace to a public norm;
- permission for an AI system to install its own powers;
- a guarantee that a committed statement is epistemically true.

Its narrower promise is more useful: when people, algorithms, and models move
through a consequential body of work, the forms, acts, seams, and results can
remain inspectable — and the methods that prove useful can become portable
without erasing the context from which they came.
