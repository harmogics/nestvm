# SDLC — the development lifecycle of this system

**Status:** public foundation companion, 2026-07-18 — the closing
integration of the phase that built the law layer. This document is
deliberately bind-shaped: it gathers the phase's scope, publishes one
integration, keeps its open questions open, and awaits acceptance
(Art. 9) like any candidate. Three altitudes now stand together:
**law** ([CONSTITUTION](./CONSTITUTION.md)) — what is protected;
**procedure** ([PROTOCOL_DEV](./PROTOCOL_DEV.md)) — how work flows;
**understanding** (this file) — why the whole moves as one.

## 1. The statement

The development of this system is not a line from requirements to
maintenance. It is **a study session whose machine is us**: a cycle on an
append-only history, run under a constitution, checked through one
seven-phase ethic, worked by humans and models sharing one language, and
aimed beyond itself — at capabilities that ripen into seeds a wider
field can grow. The classical SDLC is our named departure point: where
it iterates over mutable state, ours appends attributable acts; where it
separates building from testing, ours interrogates meaning at every
altitude; where it ends at deployment, ours ends at a threshold — the
moment a capability leaves as a seed, carrying experience without code.

## 2. The lifecycle

```text
want ──► seed (capabilities/: the want in distributable form)
   ──► proposal (spec/: the working canvas — seven phases,
        Truth → Responsibility, artefact per phase)
   ──► the gate (the human's go — never implied)
   ──► code (the radial tree; the construction paradigm;
        conventions as the shared language)
   ──► verification (the instrument ladder, cheapest first:
        fractal test → routing/DAG → manifests → tsc/parity/diff →
        personas → fixtures and live smoke → the seven matrices)
   ──► landing records (ADRs, refimpl chapters, lids, worked examples)
   ──► seed updated (learned detail back-propagated; version, status)
   ──► proposal retired (spec/history/ — the development's own log)
        … and the next want finds the seed waiting.
```

Every arrow appends; nothing rewrites. Corrections are new dated acts;
the history of the development is governed by the same Article 1 as the
history it develops.

## 3. The law and the ethic in the loop

The constitution is not documentation beside the work — it is the
work's *collection declaration*: what may enter as a change is fixed in
advance, and every change is interrogated through the seven Harmogics
phases, which serve twice — as the design phases of the protocol and as
the check dimensions of verification (Vol. 14 §4). Drift detection is
this SDLC's quality discipline generalised: not only "does the code
pass" but "does the meaning still trace" — result-level, architecture-
level, persona-level, with the fractal test as the one-question gate on
everything. Pretending remains the only non-conformance.

## 4. The developers

Development here is itself **declared form, supplied content**: the
protocol, the templates, the manifests and conventions fix the forms;
humans and models supply content inside them. The human holds what
cannot be delegated — purpose, pivots, consequential selection, the
gates, acceptance, attestation (Art. 9). A model proposes, checks
against the sources, implements, and reports honestly — including its
own misses; its internal movement between vectors is disciplined
(CLAUDE.md, Vector choice), its challenges verified before defended.
Several models can work the same body because the language is shared and
explicit: the reading cascade (shape → layers → lids → headers), the
conventions, the anatomy of files. No author is special; every author is
accountable to the same ladder.

## 5. The ecosystem horizon

A capability's lifecycle is longer than its code's. Inside the
repository it walks the cycle; at maturity it approaches the
**threshold**: de-identification, rights review, versioning — and leaves
as a seed (guild or atomic), which a target system *grows* through its
own assembly: admit → sow → ground → seat. What travels is experience
in declared forms — questions, angles, thresholds, rubrics, schemas,
fixtures, provenance — never code, never raw history (history is not
publication). The guild principles close the arc: development that
began as one system studying its machine ends as a field exchanging
figures of work, each entering fresh contexts under fresh admission,
"portable without erasing the context from which they came"
(PHILOSOPHY, the narrower promise).

## 6. Honest incompleteness as method

This SDLC never presents itself as finished: ledgers hold the known
debts (retired opportunistically, each at its named occasion);
"deliberately not now" lists guard scope; open-question registers keep
what is genuinely open, with owners; settled-unfinished is a valid state
of the development itself. Completion exists only where a declared
result contract and the human's judgement say it exists — for the
product's sessions and for our own phases alike.

## 7. The reflexive close

The system's first study subject was the machine itself; this phase
extended the reflexion one level: the development now runs the figure it
builds — seeds as subjects, proposals as scenes, implementation as
grounding, records as publication, `spec/history/` as the log. When the
history folder fills, the workbench will be able to study its own
development the way it studies its machine — the bootstrap closed in
both directions. That is the understanding this bind publishes:
**the system and its making are one figure at two radii.**
